Public Health and Foreign Aid Ties

We explore the association between countries' statistics associated with health quality and their reception of foreign aid from the US. We investigate whether the US decides where its foreign aid goes based on humanitaran reasons, and whether the aid is efficacious. We hypothesized that the US may base a significant part of its aid decisions on humanitarian reasons, but the rest may be based on national security concerns.
